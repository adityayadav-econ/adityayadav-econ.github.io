01 · About

About Me

I am a PhD Scholar in Economics at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Bhopal, where my research sits at the intersection of climate economics and macroeconomic policy. My work investigates how climate shocks propagate through financial systems and shape inflation, growth, and sovereign risk - with a focus on evidence-based policy frameworks for sustainable development.

I hold an Integrated Master's degree in Economics from the University of Hyderabad, where I developed strong foundations in economic theory, quantitative methods, and applied research. My broader methodological toolkit spans applied econometrics, spatial analysis, production efficiency analysis, and panel data methods, applied to questions in development economics and fiscal sustainability.

I am keen on contributing to research that meaningfully bridges climate change and economic policymaking to support resilient, sustainable development in emerging economies - particularly in the Indian context.

03 · Research

Research Interests

My primary research interests lie in the macroeconomics of climate change, green finance, spatial inequality, and fiscal policy. I have undertaken exploratory work across several of these areas, with a view to developing more focused research questions during my PhD.

01
Climate Risk & Macroeconomic Policy

Interested in how climate shocks interact with macroeconomic stability, inflation, growth, and financial systems - particularly the design of fiscal and monetary policy responses under climate uncertainty in emerging markets.

02
Spatial Inequality Across Indian States

Analysing per capita NSDP inequality using GeoDa, QGIS, and Python. Methods include Global Moran's I, LISA cluster maps, sigma/beta convergence, Chow structural break test, and EKC analysis linking income to forest cover change.

03
Fiscal Sustainability & Reaction Functions

Interested in fiscal reaction function frameworks and how governments in advanced and emerging economies respond to debt dynamics. Have engaged with System GMM panel approaches in this context as part of coursework.

04
Green Finance & India's Climate Transition

Interested in how India's financial system - commercial banks, DFIs, and private capital - can support the low-carbon transition. Engaged with themes of green banking, blended finance, and the structural challenges of redirecting capital toward sustainable sectors.
